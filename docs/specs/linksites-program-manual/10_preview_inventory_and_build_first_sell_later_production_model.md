## 1. Purpose of this section

This section defines how LiNKsites uses real, personalized website previews as a sales asset before an SMB purchases the service.

The build-first, sell-later strategy is based on a simple commercial belief: a prospect is more likely to understand and value a website that already appears to belong to its business than a report, abstract promise, or generic portfolio example.

The strategy creates upfront production cost. LiNKsites manages that cost by:

- Investing at different proof levels according to opportunity quality
- Starting from reusable foundations rather than blank projects
- Using deterministic executors wherever possible
- Reusing governed design, component, copy, and media patterns
- Tracking actual production cost
- Preserving the reusable value of unsold previews
- Refactoring and offering suitable foundations to later prospects
- Increasing investment only when lead quality or engagement justifies it

This section turns that commercial thesis into a governed, measurable production system.

## 2. Core doctrine

1. **The proof is a website.** Levels 1 through 4 must provide visible website proof. A report may assist internal qualification or outreach, but it is not the primary LiNKsites sales deliverable.
2. **Prebuild cost is a managed acquisition investment.** The goal is not to eliminate all upfront cost; it is to make the smallest rational investment that creates compelling proof and retains reusable value.
3. **Unsold does not automatically mean wasted.** A prospect adaptation may be cleansed, refactored, and reused if rights, fit, quality, and isolation requirements are satisfied.
4. **Proof investment follows authority.** An agent cannot raise the proof level or exceed the budget because it believes a site could be better.
5. **Proof level is not paid tier.** The amount built before a sale and the product purchased after a sale are independent dimensions.
6. **Every preview is isolated.** Reused foundations do not justify mixing prospect identities, assets, forms, analytics, or data.
7. **Inventory is measured as a portfolio.** LiNKsites should know what it has, what it cost, where it fits, how often it has been used, and what outcomes it has produced.
8. **Quality remains real at every level.** A lower proof level means narrower scope, not a broken or embarrassing website.
9. **Commercial outcome and technical disposition are separate.** Sales decides what happened to the opportunity; LiNKsites decides what happens to the reusable technical asset under an authorized outcome instruction.
10. **The thesis must prove itself with data.** Initial assumptions are hypotheses until actual cost, conversion, reuse, and maintenance evidence exists.

## 3. Build-first, sell-later definition

**Build first** means that LiNKtrend authorizes LiNKsites to produce an actual prospect-specific website preview before confirmed purchase.

**Sell later** means that the LiNKtrend Sales Program uses the preview as proof during outreach, qualification, demonstration, objection handling, or follow-up and seeks a paid order for the managed LiNKsites service.

The preview is not automatically:

- A live customer website
- Customer-approved content
- A completed paid tier
- A transfer of the reusable foundation
- Proof of a contractual relationship
- Permission to activate a customer domain
- Permission to process live form submissions
- Permission to treat public research as customer-confirmed truth

After verified payment and a valid commercial activation package, LiNKsites enters a separate customer finalization and launch lifecycle.

## 4. Why the model can remain viable in the AI era

AI can now generate website code quickly, but generation speed alone does not determine commercial viability. LiNKsites must optimize the full cost of:

- Research consumption
- Factual verification
- Design decisions
- Copy and media production
- Frontend composition
- CMS mapping
- Preview deployment
- Quality assurance
- Customer finalization
- Hosting and maintenance
- Future changes
- Failure recovery

A frontier agent generating a new application for every lead may appear fast while creating inconsistent code, large token cost, duplicated testing, maintenance drift, security risk, and difficult recycling.

LiNKsites instead combines:

- Reusable Vertical Kits
- Tier Specifications
- Design Intelligence
- Registered components
- Reusable Site Foundations
- Deterministic assembly
- Targeted, bounded agent judgment
- Versioned working and CMS content

This architecture allows AI to improve personalization and evaluation without making unconstrained generation the most expensive and least predictable part of every sale.

## 5. Operational meaning of Preview Inventory

**Preview Inventory** is the governed portfolio of reusable website-production assets and prospect preview instances used in the build-first sales process.

It includes:

- Available Reusable Site Foundations
- Reserved foundations
- Prospect adaptations in production
- Sales-ready previews
- Presented previews
- Held previews
- Unsold adaptations awaiting cleansing
- Cleansed foundations ready for reuse
- Foundation and preview lineage
- Cost, quality, engagement, and outcome records
- Refresh, repair, deprecation, and retirement queues

The term “inventory” describes operational control and reuse. Whether any cost qualifies for a specific financial-accounting treatment is a separate accounting decision and must not be inferred by engineering. Odoo and approved finance policy remain authoritative for accounting records.

## 6. Inventory object model

### 6.1 Reusable Site Foundation

A prospect-neutral, tested composition eligible for adaptation under declared Vertical Kit, tier, design, language, media, and conversion constraints.

### 6.2 Foundation Version

An immutable-in-effect release of a foundation with a pinned Site Assembly Manifest, platform release, Adaptation Contract, tests, provenance, and cost baseline.

### 6.3 Foundation Reservation

A time-bounded claim on a foundation or adaptation slot for one Preview Production Request. Reservation prevents conflicts without necessarily making the foundation exclusive when safe parallel adaptations are supported.

### 6.4 Prospect Adaptation

An isolated layer containing one prospect's content, identity, assets, design resolution, configuration, and lineage.

### 6.5 Preview Deployment

A controlled rendered website associated with one prospect adaptation, proof version, URL, access policy, expiration, analytics identity, and quality receipt.

### 6.6 General Improvement

A reusable component, schema, design, test, or foundation improvement discovered during an adaptation. It becomes inventory value only after it enters and passes the appropriate capability lifecycle.

### 6.7 Outcome Record

The Sales-authorized commercial disposition and LiNKsites technical disposition of a preview.

## 7. Inventory identity and lineage

The system must preserve these relationships:

```text
Vertical Kit + Tier Variant
→ Foundation Family
→ Foundation Version
→ Reservation
→ Prospect Adaptation Version
→ Preview Deployment Version
→ Sales Outcome
→ Customer Site or Recycled Foundation
```

An object must not change identity merely because its state changes.

Lineage should answer:

- Which foundation produced this preview?
- Which prior foundation or unsold adaptation influenced the current foundation?
- Which components, design profile, content schemas, and assets were used?
- Which costs belong to reusable capability versus prospect-specific work?
- Which customer sites derive from this foundation family?
- Which prior prospect data was removed during recycling?
- Which Gate receipts admitted the asset back into inventory?

## 8. Progressive Sales Proof Levels

### 8.1 Level 0 — Research only

Level 0 represents an unqualified, weak, incomplete, or deferred opportunity for which no prospect website is produced. Sales may collect or improve research. LiNKsites has no prebuild obligation.

### 8.2 Level 1 — Minimum viable personalized preview

Level 1 is for a plausible prospect. It must show an actual personalized website, normally using a highly reusable foundation with narrow adaptation.

It may include:

- Prospect identity
- Verified principal offering
- Essential contact or action pathway
- A limited but coherent page or homepage experience
- Suitable brand or governed design adaptation
- Safe preview behavior

It must not be reduced to a written report.

### 8.3 Level 2 — Personalized homepage and essential experience

Level 2 is for a good prospect. It normally demonstrates a more complete homepage and essential visitor journey, with stronger content, media, and conversion fit.

### 8.4 Level 3 — Near-complete, substantially personalized site

Level 3 is for a strong prospect. It normally shows most of the important site experience expected for the recommended offer, while retaining any clearly recorded production-finalization requirements.

### 8.5 Level 4 — Complete sale-ready preview

Level 4 is for the highest-probability or highest-value opportunities. It is intended to be a compelling, substantially complete demonstration suitable for closing the sale, potentially produced by refactoring and upgrading an unsold Level 2 or Level 3 asset.

### 8.6 Proof Specifications

Each level must have a versioned Proof Specification defining:

- Maximum authorized investment or budget class
- Required degree of personalization
- Expected page and section coverage
- Content and media depth
- Permitted placeholders
- Required integrations or demonstrations
- Preview access and expiration
- Quality Gates
- Sales handoff contents
- Upgrade behavior

Exact monetary budgets and page counts remain configurable and are not decided by these definitions.

## 9. Proof level is independent from paid tier

The system must keep these separate:

```text
proof_level
recommended_tier_id
quoted_tier_id
purchased_tier_id
active_tier_id
```

A Level 1 preview may demonstrate the value of a Premium product without prebuilding the entire Premium entitlement. A Level 4 preview does not grant Enterprise service. The paid order and Tier Specification determine what LiNKsites must finalize, host, maintain, and support.

## 10. Prebuild authorization

A Preview Production Request must contain evidence of authority for:

- Proof level
- Budget or budget class
- Campaign
- Prospect identity
- Target product
- Recommended tier where available
- Deadline
- Expiration
- Any exceptional foundation investment

Authorization may be produced automatically by a policy owned by the Sales Program, within delegated limits. Higher cost, weak evidence, unsupported verticals, or large new-capability investment may require OpenClaw-mediated or human review.

LiNKsites validates authority but does not create commercial authority for itself.

## 11. Authorization factors

Sales may use factors such as:

- Lead quality and confidence
- Fit with the LiNKsites customer profile
- Evidence that the current web presence is weak or missing
- Business activity and reachability
- Estimated product value
- Recommended tier
- Reusable foundation match
- Existing unsold preview fit
- Estimated adaptation cost
- Probability of reuse if unsold
- Campaign priority
- Prospect engagement
- Sales capacity

The exact scoring model belongs to the Sales Program. LiNKsites provides technical fit, inventory availability, and cost estimates.

## 12. Plain-English investment logic

The four paid-proof levels exist because not every lead justifies the same upfront spend.

- For a plausible lead, show enough real website value to create interest using a strong reusable starting point.
- For a better lead, personalize more of the important visitor experience.
- For a strong lead, invest in something close to the expected final site.
- For the best lead, present a complete sale-ready demonstration.

If the prospect responds positively, invest more through an authorized upgrade. If the prospect does not buy, recover what is reusable rather than treating the entire preview as disposable.

## 13. Foundation coverage strategy

Inventory should provide adequate, not unlimited, coverage across:

- Target Vertical Kits and subverticals
- Provisional paid tiers
- Conversion profiles
- Content-depth profiles
- Media-availability profiles
- Design families
- Supported languages and scripts
- Single-location and multi-location patterns
- Standard integration needs

Coverage targets must reflect actual lead supply and campaign plans. The Program should not build dozens of visually different foundations for a vertical with no qualified leads. It should also avoid repeatedly authorizing expensive new builds because one active campaign lacks a basic reusable foundation.

## 14. Inventory demand forecasting

LiNKsites should receive aggregated demand signals from Sales, including:

- Lead count by vertical and subvertical
- Lead quality distribution
- Geographic and language distribution
- Recommended tier distribution
- Common website deficiencies
- Frequent integration needs
- Campaign schedule
- Expected outreach volume

It combines these with:

- Available foundation coverage
- Adaptation queue
- Conversion history
- Reuse rate
- Rejection reasons
- Staleness and repair backlog
- Production capacity

The result may trigger foundation creation, refresh, consolidation, or retirement.

## 15. Foundation matching

Matching begins with hard eligibility filters:

- Active or otherwise eligible foundation state
- Vertical and subvertical compatibility
- Tier Variant compatibility
- Proof-level suitability
- Platform release availability
- Locale and script support
- Required conversion path
- Component and integration capability
- Media fit
- Rights and provenance eligibility
- Current quality status
- Reservation availability

Eligible foundations are ranked by:

- Prospect and brand fit
- Adaptation cost
- Time to sales-ready state
- Quality and performance history
- Prior engagement and conversion evidence
- Visual distinctiveness in the campaign
- Content and media compatibility
- Reuse potential if the lead does not convert
- Maintenance burden

The output is a Foundation Match Proposal with primary and alternate options, estimated cost, expected changes, limitations, and reason codes.

## 16. Existing inventory versus new construction

When no perfect match exists, LiNKsites decides among:

1. Use the best eligible foundation with direct substitutions.
2. Use the foundation with bounded composition changes.
3. Refresh or repair an existing foundation.
4. Refactor a cleansed unsold preview or related foundation.
5. Upgrade an existing lower-proof adaptation.
6. Build a new foundation because expected reuse justifies it.
7. Reduce or defer the requested proof.
8. Reject the assignment as infeasible within authority.

The decision must compare total cost, time, suitability, quality, and residual value—not only the number of source-code changes.

## 17. Foundation build authorization

A new foundation is more reusable investment than ordinary prospect adaptation. Authorization should consider:

- Number and quality of similar leads
- Strategic importance of the Vertical Kit
- Missing inventory coverage
- Expected number of reuses
- Available components and neutral assets
- Initial build cost
- Expected future adaptation cost
- Expected maintenance cost
- Time until campaign use
- Capability gaps
- Probability that the foundation remains useful if the first prospect rejects it

A new foundation must enter the foundation lifecycle and pass Inventory Admission. It cannot be disguised as an untracked one-off preview expense.

## 18. Reservation and concurrency

A reservation record should identify:

- Foundation and version
- Preview request
- Prospect
- Reservation mode
- Start and expiration
- Adaptation slot or branch identity
- Conflicting reservations
- Owning workflow
- Release conditions

Reservation may be:

- Exclusive for a defined period
- Non-exclusive where isolated parallel adaptations are supported
- Capacity-limited when a foundation family has a campaign or similarity policy

Expired reservations should release automatically only after confirming that no active Run or deployment depends on them.

## 19. Preview production queue

Accepted requests should be prioritized through explicit policy. Queue inputs may include:

- Sales priority
- Proof level
- Deadline
- Estimated value
- Existing engagement
- Foundation availability
- Cost to readiness
- Campaign timing
- Reuse value
- Current executor capacity
- Blocked dependency

Urgency must not bypass identity, research, rights, isolation, or quality Gates. The queue should expose why an item was prioritized and whether it is aging beyond its commercial usefulness.

Every preview and reusable asset should have attributable cost records.

### 20.1 Foundation costs

- Vertical research allocation where applicable
- Design and component work
- Neutral copy and media patterns
- Assembly
- CMS mapping
- Testing
- Initial deployment fixtures
- Capability maintenance

### 20.2 Adaptation costs

- Research consumption
- Planning
- Copy transformation or creation
- Media selection, generation, or processing
- Design resolution
- Deterministic compute
- Agent/model tokens
- CMS working and draft operations
- Preview deployment

### 20.3 Upgrade costs

- Prospect-data cleansing
- Asset replacement
- Neutralization
- Revalidation

Costs must reference Issues, Runs, executors, resource usage, and allocation policy.

## 21. Reusable versus prospect-specific cost

The ledger should distinguish:

- **Reusable capability investment:** creates or improves a Kit, component, design record, schema, test, or foundation useful beyond one lead.
- **Prospect-specific adaptation:** exists only to personalize proof for one prospect.
- **Mixed work:** has both reusable and prospect-specific outputs and requires an explicit allocation method.

Agents must not relabel failed prospect work as reusable value without a validated reusable output.

## 22. Residual value

Residual value is an operational estimate of the future usefulness of an unsold asset. It may consider:

- Foundation quality and freshness
- Number of similar qualified leads
- Cost of cleansing and refactoring
- Rights and reuse restrictions
- Conversion and engagement history
- Similarity saturation
- Required platform migration
- Maintenance burden
- Expected remaining life

Residual value is not assumed to equal historical spend. A high-cost but unsuitable preview may have little remaining value. A modest foundation with broad demand may retain most of its operational usefulness.

## 23. Decision-support economics

The Program may use decision-support measures such as:

```text
prospect preview expected contribution
= estimated probability of sale × expected gross contribution
− incremental preview and finalization cost
```

and:

```text
risk-adjusted preview cost
= current preview cost
− expected reusable residual value
```

These are analytical structures, not approved permanent formulas. Probabilities, contribution, time horizon, and residual estimates must be calibrated from evidence and reviewed for bias.

The Sales Program owns overall acquisition economics. LiNKsites supplies truthful production, reuse, fulfilment, hosting, and support cost inputs.

## 24. Illustrative reuse economics

If an initial site costs USD 30 and a later refactor costs USD 5, rejection by the first prospect does not necessarily destroy USD 30. The cleansed foundation may be offered to another suitable lead after additional low-cost work and may be refactored several times before conversion.

The figures illustrate the intended logic only. They are not target costs, price commitments, or industry benchmarks. LiNKsites must measure real values by vertical, proof level, foundation family, executor policy, and time period.

## 25. Preview isolation and safe serving

Every preview deployment must have:

- Stable preview ID
- Prospect ID
- Versioned Site Assembly Manifest
- Versioned Payload draft or preview content reference
- Separate analytics identity
- Safe form and conversion behavior
- Access policy
- Expiration policy
- Indexing policy
- Deployment and quality receipt

The preview must not:

- Use another prospect's data
- Accept production submissions into an unconfigured destination
- Expose secrets or working records
- Be mistaken by search engines for the customer's official production site where policy forbids that
- Continue indefinitely after its opportunity and retention period have ended
- Become a live customer site merely because payment occurs

## 26. Preview URL and access policy

Possible access modes include:

- Private authenticated preview
- Unlisted signed or difficult-to-guess preview
- Time-limited link
- Controlled public demonstration

The final mode depends on Sales strategy, legal policy, indexing risk, and user experience. The system must support policy-driven controls rather than one global hard-coded choice.

Access logs alone do not prove the prospect was presented the preview. Sales supplies the commercial presentation event.

## 27. Analytics and engagement

Preview analytics may capture policy-approved events such as:

- Page view
- Return visit
- Section engagement
- Call-to-action interaction
- Video or gallery interaction
- Form demonstration interaction
- Link follow-through

Technical telemetry and commercial engagement analytics should remain distinct.

Analytics must use a preview-specific identity so activity cannot contaminate customer production analytics or another prospect's record.

Engagement evidence may justify:

- Sales follow-up
- Proof upgrade
- Preview hold extension
- Specific content improvement

An isolated metric should not automatically trigger large spending. Upgrade policy should combine engagement with Sales state, cost, and authorization.

## 28. Preview Ready Package

When a preview passes required Gates, LiNKsites returns a package to Sales containing:

- Preview ID and version
- Prospect and opportunity references
- Preview URL and access instructions
- Proof level
- Recommended tier and product references
- What the preview demonstrates
- Known limitations
- Provisional or production-replacement elements
- Safe talking points and approved capability claims
- Expiration
- Analytics reference
- Cost summary
- Quality receipt
- Technical contact or exception path

Sales should not have to infer whether a form works, which content is provisional, or what the customer would receive after purchase.

## 29. Presentation state

A preview becomes `presented` only from an attributable Sales event. The event should identify:

- Opportunity
- Preview version
- Presentation time
- Channel
- Sales actor or executor
- Follow-up state

This prevents an automated health check, crawler visit, or internal QA view from being mistaken for prospect engagement.

## 30. Engagement-based upgrades

An upgrade from one proof level to another requires:

- A valid Sales upgrade request
- New proof level and budget authority
- Updated research if needed
- Revised Site Specification
- Updated Prospect Adaptation Contract
- Cost estimate
- Foundation and capability feasibility
- New Gate profile

The system preserves the earlier version and its cost. It must not overwrite Level 1 history with the Level 3 result.

Upgrade may involve completing more pages, improving media, adding supported functionality, deepening copy, or moving to a better foundation. It should not add work unrelated to the sale merely because the preview is already open.

## 31. Preview outcome states

Sales may communicate an outcome such as:

- Engaged
- Inactive
- Follow-up scheduled
- Holding
- Rejected
- Lost to competitor
- Not currently ready
- Invalid lead
- Converted
- Expired
- Do not contact

LiNKsites maps the commercial event to a technical action without redefining the CRM state.

Technical dispositions include:

- Keep current preview active within policy
- Extend hold
- Upgrade
- Lock for conversion
- Expire deployment
- Cleanse and recycle
- Repair
- Refresh
- Deprecate or retire
- Preserve evidence and remove active serving

## 32. Holding policy

A preview may be held when the opportunity remains legitimate but delayed.

The hold record should define:

- Reason
- Sales authority
- Start and expiration
- Serving state
- Data and asset retention
- Refresh requirements
- Cost of continued hosting
- Reassessment event

Holding cannot become indefinite by default. Stale previews create cost, security surface, outdated claims, and inventory confusion.

## 33. Conversion lock

When Sales sends a valid conversion instruction based on Stripe-confirmed payment and the corresponding Odoo commercial record, LiNKsites locks the relevant preview version and foundation relationship for customer finalization.

Conversion lock prevents:

- Recycling while fulfilment is active
- Reassigning prospect-specific assets
- Updating the preview underneath the order
- Losing the exact version shown and sold

Conversion does not automatically publish the preview. Paid finalization verifies customer facts, replaces provisional assets, completes purchased scope, configures production integrations and domain, obtains required approval, and passes launch Gates.

## 34. Recycling an unsold preview

Recycling begins only from an authorized Sales outcome or expiration policy.

The process is:

1. Stop or restrict the preview according to policy.
2. Snapshot the accepted outcome, costs, analytics, and lineage.
3. Classify each asset and improvement as prospect-specific, reusable, restricted, or disposable.
4. Remove prospect identity, copy, contact details, metadata, structured data, media, form destinations, analytics, and routing.
5. Restore neutral content and configuration.
6. Promote general improvements through their capability lifecycles.
7. Recalculate adaptation requirements and residual value.
8. Run prospect-data cleansing and quality Gates.
9. Return the foundation to available inventory, create a new version, or route it to repair, refresh, deprecation, or retirement.

Deletion and retention details remain subject to final policy, but active reuse must never carry forward inappropriate prospect data.

## 35. Cleansing evidence

The Cleansing Gate should inspect:

- Visible page text
- Metadata
- Structured data
- Alt text and captions
- Asset filenames and metadata
- Logos and icons
- Links and telephone actions
- Form endpoints
- Analytics and tracking IDs
- CMS records
- Supabase working records
- Environment configuration
- Preview routes
- Caches and generated artifacts
- Screenshots and fixtures intended for reuse

The result is a signed or checksummed Recycle Receipt identifying what was removed, what was retained, which tests passed, and the new inventory state.

## 36. Refactoring for a stronger lead

An unsold site initially produced for a Level 2 or Level 3 prospect may be cleansed and refactored for a later Level 4 prospect when:

- The Vertical Kit and tier fit
- The foundation is technically and visually current
- The new lead's business and brand fit the composition
- Previous prospect material is fully isolated or removed
- Media and design rights permit reuse
- Similarity policy allows it
- The estimated refactor is lower risk and cost than another approach
- The new proof authority covers the work

The new prospect receives a new adaptation, manifest, preview ID, analytics identity, and Site Specification. It is not a renamed copy of the prior prospect's deployment.

## 37. Repair, refresh, deprecation, and retirement

A foundation may leave available inventory because of:

- Security defect
- Accessibility failure
- Broken integration
- Framework or platform incompatibility
- Stale visual treatment
- Poor performance
- License or provenance concern
- Excessive similarity or overuse
- Repeated prospect rejection
- High adaptation or maintenance cost
- Vertical Kit change
- Poor conversion relative to alternatives

Severe defects trigger suspension. Repair addresses a defect while preserving the general foundation. Refresh modernizes or repositions it. Deprecation blocks new assignment while derived sites are migrated. Retirement ends future use and preserves history.

## 38. Portfolio balancing

Inventory should be balanced across commercial need rather than raw quantity.

Useful portfolio measures include:

- Coverage rate for qualified leads
- Percentage matched without new foundation work
- Average time to sales-ready preview
- Foundations by active vertical and tier
- Foundation reuse distribution
- Style and composition concentration
- Reservation utilization
- Stale or suspended share
- Unused foundation age
- Conversion rate by family
- Cost per conversion by family

A large inventory with low coverage or high staleness is not healthy. A small inventory that repeatedly forces new construction is also not healthy.

## 39. Similarity and saturation

LiNKsites must track whether a foundation, design profile, or composition is being shown too often in one campaign, market, or visible business network.

The system may:

- Prefer a compatible alternate foundation
- Rotate approved component and design profiles
- Refresh media patterns
- Create a new foundation when demand justifies it
- Limit parallel assignments

Similarity controls must not override suitability, quality, or cost irrationally. Thresholds should be calibrated with Sales evidence.

## 40. Autonomous operation

Routine inventory operations should be autonomous:

- Register validated foundations
- Calculate coverage
- Match and rank eligible foundations
- Reserve and release capacity
- Track costs and state
- Expire reservations
- Detect stale previews
- Trigger refresh or cleansing Issues
- Process authorized holds and upgrades
- Generate portfolio reports
- Detect abnormal leakage or quality signals

OpenClaw acts as an external operational overseer for exceptions, ambiguous economic decisions, repeated failures, or authority escalation. It is not required for normal matching, serving, or state transitions.

## 43. Anti-gaming and interpretation safeguards

Metrics must not encourage harmful behavior.

Examples:

- Low cost achieved by producing weak previews is not success.
- High reuse achieved by showing every prospect the same unsuitable site is not success.
- High engagement from internal QA traffic is not commercial engagement.
- A conversion should not be credited to a foundation without considering Sales channel and lead quality.
- A preview should not remain active to improve presentation counts after the opportunity ended.
- Residual value should not be inflated to hide unsuccessful spending.

Metric definitions, attribution windows, exclusions, and versions must be explicit.

## 46. Repository audit questions

The repository and data audit must determine:

1. Whether a Preview Inventory service or schema already exists.
2. Whether foundations, templates, demos, and prospect previews are distinguishable.
3. Whether proof levels are implemented and whether Levels 1–4 produce actual websites.

## 49. Acceptance criteria

This part of LiNKsites is adequately defined and implemented when:

1. Levels 1 through 4 always produce visible website proof rather than only reports.
2. Every preview request has proof-level and budget authority from Sales.
3. Proof level, recommended tier, quoted tier, purchased tier, and active tier remain separate.
4. Foundations, reservations, prospect adaptations, preview deployments, and outcomes have separate identities.
5. Foundation selection uses hard compatibility filters and attributable ranking.
6. New foundation work requires explicit reusable-investment authorization.
7. Parallel adaptations cannot overwrite or expose each other's data.
8. Costs are traceable to reusable capability, prospect adaptation, upgrade, finalization, and recycling work.
9. Residual value is evidence-based and cannot be equated automatically with historical spend.
10. Every sales-ready preview has safe access, routing, forms, analytics, expiration, and quality evidence.
11. A Sales presentation event is distinct from a page view or internal QA visit.
12. Engagement-based upgrades require new proof authority and versioned production contracts.
13. Conversion locks the exact preview version but does not bypass customer finalization and launch Gates.
14. Unsold previews can be cleansed, refactored, and reused without retaining prior prospect identity or restricted assets.
15. A Recycle Receipt proves cleansing and return-to-inventory eligibility.
16. Inventory coverage, cost, reuse, quality, engagement, conversion, and maintenance are measurable.
17. Stale, defective, overused, restricted, or uneconomic foundations can be suspended or retired.
18. Routine inventory management is autonomous, while OpenClaw supports exceptions outside the runtime.
19. Sales, LiNKsites, Odoo, and Stripe retain their separate authorities.
20. The business thesis can be tested using actual pilot evidence rather than optimistic assumptions.

## 50. Governing conclusion

The build-first, sell-later model is the defining commercial mechanism of LiNKsites. Its strength is that the prospect sees the proposed outcome rather than being asked to imagine it. Its risk is uncontrolled upfront cost. Preview Inventory is the system that converts that risk into governed, reusable investment.

LiNKsites should begin with the strongest suitable foundation, personalize only as much as the opportunity justifies, present a real and credible website, and increase investment when evidence supports doing so. When a prospect purchases, the exact preview becomes the controlled basis of customer finalization. When a prospect does not purchase, LiNKsites preserves what is genuinely reusable, removes what is prospect-specific, and returns a validated foundation to the portfolio.

The intended advantage compounds over time: more tested foundations, better matching, lower adaptation cost, stronger evidence, and faster proof. That compounding value exists only if the Program rigorously tracks identity, rights, cost, quality, lineage, engagement, and reuse. Without those controls, prebuilding becomes speculative custom work; with them, it becomes the scalable sales-production engine at the center of LiNKsites.
